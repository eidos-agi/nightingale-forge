Nightingale reads the wreckage and writes the warning on the door.

Named for Florence Nightingale, who didn't just nurse soldiers — she studied WHY they were dying, invented data visualization to prove it, and changed medicine because the evidence was undeniable.

This forge teaches agents to study failures systematically: read session transcripts, extract incidents, catalog them with structured evidence, and surface unprotected failure modes before they repeat.

Nightingale is upstream of any encoder of "don't do this again." The incident corpus feeds:
- **StepProof** — turns gaps into runbook ceremony gates
- **forge-forge** — hardens forges themselves against the patterns incidents reveal
- **skills / runbooks generally** — incidents become new guardrails or skill updates
- **cockpit-mcp** — incidents surface as continuity artifacts at takeoff/land

The output is not a report. It's a living incident corpus that every downstream encoder consults. Every study run makes the corpus richer. Every report — whether it's a runbook gap, a forge pattern, or a skill update — tightens a different fence. The forge is the machine; the incidents are the compound interest.
